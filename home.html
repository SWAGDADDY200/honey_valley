    Benefits of Honey

    Rich in Antioxidants

    Many of use enjoy honey and incorporate it into our daily diet, whether as a
    natural sugar substitute for our coffee or simply treat. But
    specific history varietals known as "active " honeys have also been utilized
    throughout history for their antibacterial and medicinal properties.

    Improves Cholesterol

    Studies have shown that consuming honey lowers LDL (bad) cholesterol
    by 6-11% and lowers triglyceride levels by as much as 11%. Honey may
    also increase HDL (good) cholesterol by about 3%

    Natural Cough Medicine

    Honey is a time honored remedy for a sore thorat. Accordingly to one study
    it can also relieve coughs more effectively than OTC medictaitons that
    contain dextromethorphan (DM), a cough suppressant. You can create
    your own remedy at home by mixing up to 2 teaspoons of honey with
    herbal tea or warm water and lemon

    Lowers Blood pressure

    Blood Pressure is an important risk factor for heart disease, and honey
    may help lower it. This is because it contains Antioxidants compounds that
    have been linked to lower blood pressure. Studies in both rats and humans
    have show modest reductions in blood pressure from consuming honey.

    Improve Heart Health

    It's high in Antioxidants, such as phenolic acids and flavonoid, which may
    support better health. Small studies in humans show honey's Antioxidants
    could help improve cholesterol levels, which could help decrease the risk
    for heart disease, although later long-term studies are needed to confirm
    those findings.

    Antibacterial properties

    Honey contains hydrogyine peroxide, which may account for some of its
    antibacterial properties. It also has a high sugar content, which can help
    stop growth of certain bacteria. Additionally, honey has a low pH level.
    This works to pull moisture away from bacteria, causing the bacteria to get
    dehydrated and die off.
